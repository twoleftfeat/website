Who we are

Two Left Feat began as a conversation between people who loved physical theatre and acrobatics, and were looking for ways to combine the two. We met through Edinburgh’s Beltane Fire Society, and though we all had fairly different backgrounds and skill sets, we realised that the combination of our particular talents could produce a kind of performance that – to our knowledge – is very rarely being produced at the moment.

Put simply, we aim to combine the visual spectacle and adrenaline fuelled stunts of acrobatics with the entertainment and delight of physical theatre. For our stage pieces we create imaginative, narrative driven shows that showcase a range of acrobalance and circus performance skills. We offer a boutique creative process that tailors these shows to the specifications of our clients – Plantasia, for instance, is a clowning show that was originally written for the Scottish Anarcho Folk Festival 2017.

In addition, we offer walkabout performances, again tailored to any theme that the client wishes, and are as at home bantering with audience members as we are tumbling about on a stage. We also conduct acrobalance workshops to suit all ages, and delight in helping participants to discover their own (sometimes previously unrealised) physical abilities.

Meet the directors

Callum Donald

My first performance took place when I was 14, and involved a choreographed martial arts fight with a Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtle in ASDA. Since winning this momentous battle, I have worked as a performer for a range of events and venues across England and Scotland. My background is in gymnastics and martial arts, and I currently perform as an acrobat, clown, dancer and fire spinner. I lead workshops in fight choreography, acrobatics, and clowning. I have not recently fought any turtles.

William Donaldson

My life as a performer began on the stages of dundee playing bass guitar in an indy rave band, I became pretty well known for being a bit crazy on the stage. My first time on the stage as a non musical performer was with the Beltane Fire Society performing as a Green Man, which hooked me into the life of performance. Soon I was performing in a fire theatre group and studying physical theatre I found this passion for performance and physical ability that had lay dormant in my psyche for so long. I continue to work with BFS along with regular performances with Pyroceltica and can also regularly be found at Brewhemia in Edinburgh as one of their in house entertainers, or at various events and festivals throughout the UK playing characters from pirates to personified chaos energy.

Sam Haddow

I am a theatre practitioner and academic. I have written and directed numerous plays that have been performed around the UK, most recently for younger audiences. I have organised and performed in Edinburgh’s Beltane Fire Society festivals, as well as performing with Two Left Feat at festivals and public events around Scotland. I teach a range of performance skills including dance, physical theatre, clowning, stand-up comedy and mask performance, and have lectured at the Universities of Nottingham, Lincoln, De Montfort, and the Central School of Speech and Drama. I am currently a lecturer and researcher in Modern and Contemporary Drama at the University of St Andrews.

Toni Mey

I have a background in dance and theatre with my mother being a ballerina and my father an actor. From when I was little I was drawn to theatrical and dance performance. Initially I focused more on ballet and standard and latin dance, but during my postgraduate time at the University of Nottingham I helped to establish a postgraduate theatre group within the New Theatre student theatre, where I took both roles on stage as well as behind the scence. Since my move to Scotland I have been an active member as a performer of the Beltane Fire Society festivals.

Past Events

Festivals

Over the summer in 2017 we have performed at a variety of Scottish festivals doing a mixture of walkabout, e.g. at Audiosoup, acorbatics workshops at Mugstock and Anarcho folk festival, as well as our first theatrical production Plantasia.

Roman v. Picts April 2017

Our very first gig was as a band of rowdy Pictish warriors for the Callendar House Romans vs. Picts run in April 2017. Our brief was to noise up the crowd and taunt the legion of Roman soldiers who had assembled for the occasion. As you can see from the photos, we took this task very seriously. In addition to putting up human pyramids, performing dynamic acrobatics and undertaking a lot of enthusiastic stage fighting, we had a great time bamming up our stuffy military opponents.

Plantasia

Plantasia was originally conceived for the Scottish Anarcho Folk festival 2017. We wanted to produce a comic, family friendly show that would explore ideas to do with ethical agricultural practices. We hit upon the idea of a gardener who attempts to grow a plant using artificial plant food, but then loses control as the plants become boisterous and destructive. We have since performed this show at a number of festivals, and at each incarnation it has been developed and refined – it now includes a group dance routine, a collapsing pyramid, people being flung high into the air and one very unfortunate audience member who becomes the object of the gardeners affections…
